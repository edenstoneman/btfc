Cressing Road Stadium
Contact Details
The home of Braintree Town FC is the Cressing Road Stadium. The capacity of Cressing Road currently stands at 4,222.
The ground has a recently extended Main Seated Stand that can accommodate 553 spectators including six wheelchair spaces.
The Cressing Road side of the ground (above) can accommodate1,130 spectators, with room for 755 under cover.
The Clubhouse End of the ground (above) has recently been upgraded with a new terrace. This end of the ground can accommodate 1,131 spectators.
The Quag End of the ground (as seen above) can accommodate approximately 1,400 spectators.
If you are looking for a function room with a kitchen, why not consider the BTFC Clubhouse? It can house up to 150 people and is Covid safe.

Directions
  1. Leave the M25 at junction 27 and head north on the M11.
  2. Leave the M11 at junction 8 and head east on the A120.
  3. Follow the A120 for 16 miles until you reach Galley's Corner roundabout.
  4. Take the first exit into Cressing Road.
  5. The entry to the ground is approximately three-quarters of a mile on your left and is sign posted.

Further Information
Braintree Town Football Club is owned and managed by Braintree Town Football Club Limited, company registration number: 02050949.

No one person or entity has overall control of the company, but the share holders that have a significant interest in the company (who own more than 10% of the authorised and issued shares) are as follows: L Harding 38.78%, K Cowell & Cowell & Cooper Limited 38.78%, V Dixon 10.33%.

The floodlighting at the Cressing Road Stadium has recently been upgraded to over 250 lux to meet the entry requirements to the Football League. This upgrade, together with six new turnstiles and a total refurbishment of the dressing rooms was completed in March 2007 at a cost of over £600,000 to meet the ground grading requirements for entry into the National League in 2011.

A further £200,000 was spent on improvements to the IronmongeryDirect Stadium, including additional turnstiles, toilets, terracing, etc., during season 2011-12 to secure The Football Association's 'A' Grade to ensure that the ground met the entry criteria for promotion to the Football League.
